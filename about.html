Who is POV Josh?

Meet Joshua Davis

My fitness journey began in 2015 in Queens, New York when I was invited to Sunday workout sessions with my cousins. I was a tall, skinny kid with a wide frame, a physique with the potential to be great for body building. I was interested in gaining weight so this peaked my interest. Within a year, I started to see a lot of progress. Using the machines and free weights gave me an understanding of how form and added resistance builds strength and muscle.

Being a college student I did not have the funds to get a full membership, so the rest of my days were spent training at the park or at home. Youtube was a highly rescourceful space for information, I started learning different ways to build muscle with just my body weight. Many of the videos I watched were of athletes within the five boroughs doing handstand pushups and muscle ups. From 2017-2019, I became focused on mastering these movements.

The more people that began asking me for tips on how to achieve my physique, the more I was compelled to find ways to help them. This was one of my major motivators when becoming a personal trainer. Working out in the park greatly influenced my personal training style. In my one on one sessions I utilized a blend of body weight and weighted movements to achieve my clients goals. Incorporating calisthenics in their training was a way for me to build a foundation for fundamental mobility and strength.

There is a stigma within the calisthenics community that some movements are more difficult for tall athletes. Being 6'2 presented a challenge that I was determined to overcome. Using the resources in my home and at the nearby park, I began my hand-balancing journey.
